Chris Ibe is an alumnae of LEAP through the Youth Leadership Programme he attended in 2007. Today he is the founder and ED of initiating African Youth Initiative on Crime Prevention. He is a recipient of the prestigious International Visitors Leadership Programme (IVLP) of the U.S State Department and a consultant. One of AYI CRIPE projects (Secondary School Intervention Project on Drugs and Substance Abuse) has been adopted in Nairobi Kenya by Healthy Thinking International, a Swedish NGO.

Describe your background and what you do today? 

My background was a disciplined and humbling one coming from a Christian family that is highly God fearing. I come from a large family of eleven. My family is loving, caring and service oriented with a passion for community and human development and this, also reflected in my life, especially in my youth development and crime prevention activities. Our first son, Dr Emmanuel Ibe was a positive influence who became a symbol of love, care and leadership that gave all of us direction in abiding in the family ideology of service to humanity and community. I am presently a social worker and counselor with special focus on youth development and crime prevention.

Why are you driving activities to end corruption and prevent crime in Nigeria ? 

Corruption is majorly responsible for the many crimes we have in Nigeria and other African Countries because the end result of it could be traced to unemployment, poor infrastructure, high crime rate, bad health system, poor education system, insecurity  and disrespect for Nigerian citizens in the committee of nations. It is my belief that all the embezzled, diverted and misappropriated funds could have been used to build factories and other infrastructure that will meaningfully engaged youth thereby denying them the opportunity of getting involved in criminal activities.

How did you start your social change journey? 

Despite being in leadership positions in the past from secondary to university, my selection by the University of Lagos Management to participate in the one year Youth Leadership Programme, (YLP) by LEAP Africa ushered another chapter of leadership responsibility in my life which actually opened me up to see clearly not just the leadership potential in me but also gave me direction on the best way to channel them.

As a student, I was really active in organizing student activities and that made me take up many leadership roles. After YLP which lasted almost a year, we were asked to come up with initiative that will provide solution to societal problems. This motivated me in initiating African Youth Initiative on Crime Prevention as a project for graduation from the YLP and we are celebrating 10 years of this initiative today. 

Having initiated and hosted the first Student Leadership and Crime Prevention at the University of Lagos which was really a huge success leading to the University presenting me with an ‘Excellent in Leadership’ award, there was a need to sustain the project considering the importance in our society.

LEAP Africa opened me up and made me realize that I have what it takes to develop young people and fight crime through prevention approach and we have reached out to 25171 youth through our programmes and many of them are now carrying out our programmes in different parts of the country and Africa. 

One of the greatest change and achievements of our organization is that Nigerians especially the law enforcement officers are beginning to talk and promote crime prevention instead of reactionary measures and we have set aside 11th March every year as International Crime Prevention Day and we are proposing this to the United Nations.

AYICRIPE turned 10 in May  2019 and that was a big milestone, what has kept you on this drive for so long? 

For me, hosting the 10th edition of National Youth Summit on Crime Prevention this year called for celebration because we have shown focus, resilience, determination and sustainability in the face of humongous challenges where getting support to host a programme like this proves almost impossible. Through this initiative we have achieved many things including accreditation with the United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC) and reached out to 25, 171 young people through our activities while the initiative have taken me and other colleagues to many countries across the world.

 

My commitment and drive which has sustained me all this while is anchored on my passion to touch the lives of young people as many as possible to enable them make an informed decision to better their lives and impact their communities.  

Are there issues that trigger and continue to demand immediate action on crime prevention? 

There are many triggers and factors that have persisted in influencing crime and social vices amongst young people especially unemployment, corruption, lack of mentors/role models, peer influence, poor family / societal values and bad leadership.

Ineffective leadership breeds corruption which leads to unemployment and renders young people unengaged thereby leading them to all manner of social interactions and contraction of friendship that may influence you negatively.

Young people need mentors that will assist them in taking life changing decisions. The absence or lack of these, breeds misguided youth populations that are vulnerable to crime activities. Poor human capacity development makes it more challenging to achieve a robust and growing society. There is therefore need for more immediate intervention by applying crime prevention strategies embodied In meaningful engagement of youth across the country. 

What support does AYICRIP need to move its agenda forward? Any thoughts on the next decade?

AYICRIP will need huge resources especially human, financial and material resources to further her project because there are more young people who are receiving the wrong information which is leading them into criminal activities and we have a huge responsibility as an organization to intervene. In carrying out these responsibilities, we need to build the capacity of our staff; we need a comfortable office as well as funding to run our programmes.
